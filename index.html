Idia Fellowship / January-mid July 2026

From Access to Agency: Co-Designing AI-Enabled Economic Empowerment Tools with Women Micro-Retailers

Contributed to a comparative socio-technical study on AI-enabled economic empowerment tools for women micro-retailers in Nairobi and Abuja. The work examined how informal traders used digital bookkeeping and credit-management tools, and translated field evidence into prototype specifications for more usable AI interfaces.

Methodology: Mixed-methods research using DCAI surveys, SokoBook usage logs, contextual-inquiry shadowing, WhatsApp voice/text diaries, paired-samples tests, and reflexive thematic analysis.

WasteWise: Household Food-Waste Management Platform

Built a Kenya-focused household workflow for managing pantry items, estimating freshness from shelf-life rules, receiving expiry alerts, recording consumption, and requesting donation or disposal. The current estimator is deterministic: it calculates expiry from item type, storage type, and purchase date, while implementation work is connecting NGO, recycling, and admin dashboards to real household actions, hardening Firebase rules, and deploying scheduled email functions.

Future work: Add a computer vision freshness-detection layer that can assess produce from images and compare visual signals against the existing shelf-life baseline.
