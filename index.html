Research Initiatives

Active Pipeline / Co-authored Working Paper

From Access to Agency: Co-Designing AI-Enabled Economic Empowerment Tools with Women Micro-Retailers

Supervisors: Dr. Elizabeth Wamicha, PhD and Dr. Wanjiru Mburu, PhD. Qhala Idia Fellowship 2026.

Developing a comparative socio-technical framework investigating interface design constraints for informal economy traders. Anchored in the Capability Approach and Affordance Theory, the study coordinates an Agile Participatory Action Research framework tracking a cohort of 50 women micro-retailers across Nairobi, Kenya, and Abuja, Nigeria.

Methodology: Mixed-methods implementation using baseline and endline Digital Confidence and Agency Index surveys, SokoBook usage logs, contextual-inquiry shadowing, and high-frequency WhatsApp voice/text diaries through Ecological Momentary Assessment. Quantitative shifts are assessed using paired-samples tests, paired with reflexive thematic analysis of qualitative data to build prototype specifications for culturally intelligent interfaces.

Technical Writing: The Gap Isn't What You Think — Reflections from the Nairobi AI Forum 2026. Read research notes on Medium ↗

WasteWise: Inventory & Shelf-Life Management System

Engineered an experimental full-stack web application designed to track and mitigate local household food waste.
